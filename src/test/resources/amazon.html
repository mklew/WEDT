5.0 out of 5 stars Excellent; does what it promises, but a bit gimmicky in places., 15 July 2012
By 
J. Chandler (London, UK) - See all my reviews
(REAL NAME)   
This review is from: Braun Oral-B TriZone 3000 Three-Mode Rechargeable Toothbrush (Packaging Varies) (Personal Care)
Customer review from the Amazon Vine Programme (What's this?)
This is an excellent toothbrush with a very powerful brushing action and a number of useful features. It comes with two brush heads, charging base, 4 head storage, travel box and at least a 1 year guarantee (see below). However, some of the features seem a bit gimmicky and equal cleaning could well be achieved with a cheaper model.

I have used an Oral B electric toothbrush for the last 3 years, and I have to say this one really is much more powerful. Where the old one chugs along like a diesel engine, this new one hums like turbine. Does it get my teeth cleaner? maybe; it definitely feels like it's doing a good job.

The much vaulted head is really good, but I don't understand why so much attention is given to it in the marketing as it's not exclusive to this brush and fits happily onto my old brush.

As with my previous model, this one times your brushing so that you give each part of your mouth equal cleaning and the 2 minutes total of brushing it needs. I've found this feature very useful, though as I said, it's not unique to this model.

It has three modes described as 'Daily clean', 'Sensitive' and 'Whitening'. What they are in reality is very fast, (comparatively) slow, and a 'slow to fast, slow to fast' repeating mode. I'm not convinced that the second two modes achieve the different kinds of cleaning they purport to, so I just use the fast mode. This does lead to the irritation of pressing the stop button, taking the toothbrush out of your mouth and finding the thing is still going, spraying toothpaste everywhere. I'll probably get used to it.

There is a built in pressure sensor which illuminates a bright red light when you brush too hard. I haven't set it off much myself, so it's of limited value for me, your mileage may vary.

The brush comes with a small charger with a European plug suitable for bathroom wall adaptors. This is of note, and one of the technological features I quite like, although again not unique to this brush. The base has a tapered plastic nubbin which fits into a similarly shaped hole in the base of the brush. There are no metal contacts; the charging is completely contactless.

It also comes with quite a large storage for 4 brush heads which will integrate with the charger. This box has a lid for the OCD amongst us. There is also a travel case supplied, with space for 2 heads; again this is somewhat larger then it needs to be, but could be useful nevertheless.

The battery life is advertised as 7 days. I can't claim to have trialled this; however it has managed two of us using it for 3 days, so I have every confidence that it would last the distance. My old brush lasts this long, and it's 3 years old, so I have confidence in Oral B's batteries. For those needing tech details the batteries are NiMH.

One thing I should point out is that there is a 2 year warranty advertised on the box, but the warranty card on the inside is for only one years warranty. Draw what you will from that.

All in all, it's a great toothbrush. It is very powerful, comes with some useful accessories and features and decent battery life. The warranty card issue points at sloppy organisation on the part of Oral B, though you probably won't need it anyway. However, if you don't want to stretch to this model, you will probably get as good a clean from a cheaper model as none of the additional features are 'must have'.

2.0 out of 5 stars Consider current retail price carefully before buying, 28 Jun. 2012
By 
Easterchick (Warwickshire) - See all my reviews
(VINE VOICE)   
This review is from: Braun Oral-B TriZone 3000 Three-Mode Rechargeable Toothbrush (Packaging Varies) (Personal Care)
Customer review from the Amazon Vine Programme (What's this?)
At the time I first reviewed this item, it was for sale at 100. A few weeks later, the price dropped to half that. I edited this review afterwards but still think you need to carefully consider if you are getting value for money, as I do think your expectations change dependent on the price.

I was looking forward to receiving this to replace my 'sonic' style toothbrush, which cleans well but isn't very good at removing stains from my front teeth.

It charges quickly - just under 3 hours but uses a 2 pin plug. However, don't expect to be able to charge it up straight away unless you have a converter plug or a 2 pin shaver socket - as a converter plug is not supplied. Luckily I had one already but if you don't you'll need to factor in buying one.

I liked the technology in the toothbrush head, which has a swivel function to reach the back teeth. It certainly cleans very well - it left my teeth feeling extremely clean and comfortable, although it didn't get rid of built up stains. However, the toothbrush heads supplied must surely have been modelled on the mouth of an elephant as they are HUGE! Again, you might find as I did that you would need to buy some smaller heads if you have an average sized or smaller mouth.

I liked the light that comes on if you press too hard - however I found the motor within the toothbrush was extremely noisy and 'rattly' - I did find it a little strange that an electric toothbrush with this price tag on it had such a cheap motor in it.

Overall, I'm afraid I wasn't that impressed, which surprised me as I thought I'd like this a lot more than I did. In my opinion, if I had paid 60-70 for this I'd probably think it was good value. At the original selling price, personally I feel it's overpriced.
